Solar System

An animated journey through our cosmic neighborhood — orbits, scales, and the quiet poetry of planets in motion.

Back to Home

About the Project

Our Solar System project is an animated simulation that visualizes the eight planets orbiting the Sun — with attention to relative orbital speeds, planetary sizes, and the characteristic colors and textures of each world. The goal was to create something both scientifically informed and aesthetically captivating — a simulation that teaches while it delights.

Behind the Project

Tools & Software

Every render, animation, and interaction in this project was built using the following tools.

🧊
Blender
3D Modeling & Animation

Used for all 3D planet modeling, texturing, orbital animations, and final render output. Cycles renderer was used for physically-based lighting.

🌐
HTML / CSS / JS
Web Development

This interactive showcase page was hand-coded — the planet cards, orbital calculator, and all animations were built without any external frameworks.

🖼️
Texture Library
Reference & Assets

High-resolution planetary surface maps sourced from a publicly available Solar System texture library were used as reference and base textures for every planet.

✏️
VS Code
Code Editor

All web code was written and organized in Visual Studio Code, using the Live Server extension to preview changes in real time during development.

Process Guide

How We Built It in Blender

A step-by-step walkthrough of how this solar system was modeled, textured, and animated.

01
Scene Setup & Scale

Open Blender and set up your scene units. We used a simplified scale where 1 Blender unit = 1 million km to keep all planets manageable. Set Cycles as renderer and configure HDRI world lighting using a deep space environment map.

Scene · Lighting · Units
step-01-scene-setup.png
02
Planet Modeling

Add a UV Sphere for each planet (Shift+A → Mesh → UV Sphere). Set segments to 64×64 for smooth curvature. Scale each sphere proportionally based on real diameter ratios — Jupiter should be ~11× the diameter of Earth.

Modeling · UV Sphere · Scale
step-02-planet-modeling.png
03
Texturing with Shader Nodes

Apply a Principled BSDF to each planet. Import texture maps and plug them into Base Color, Roughness, and Normal inputs. For the Sun, use an Emission shader with an orange-white gradient. Add a Point Light inside the Sun for scene illumination.

Shading · Nodes · Textures
step-03-shader-nodes.png
04
Orbital Paths & Animation

Use Empty objects as orbit centers. Parent each planet to its Empty, then animate the Empty's Z-rotation from 0° to 360°. Use orbital period ratios for relative keyframe counts. Enable the Graph Editor to set linear interpolation on all rotation curves.

Animation · Keyframes · Empties
step-04-animation.png
05
Saturn's Ring System

Create a flat disc (Add → Mesh → Circle, Extrude and scale inward to form a ring). Apply an alpha-transparent texture for the ice particle look using Principled BSDF with Transmission. Tilt the ring ~27° to match Saturn's axial tilt, then parent it to the planet.

Modeling · Transparency · Rings
step-05-saturn-rings.png
06
Camera, Rendering & Export

Position your camera with a wide-angle lens (18–24mm) for the full system overview, or animate it for a dramatic flythrough. Set resolution to 1920×1080, enable Motion Blur, render to PNG frames, then assemble in Blender's Video Sequence Editor.

Camera · Render · Export

The Solar System

Celestial Bodies & Orbital Dynamics

Every body in our solar system has its own rhythm, personality, and place in the cosmic dance.

The Sun
Star · Center of the Solar System

The Sun is the gravitational anchor of our entire solar system — a massive ball of hot plasma generating energy through nuclear fusion at its core. It accounts for 99.86% of the solar system's total mass and its gravity keeps all eight planets locked in their orbital paths.

  • Diameter1,392,700 km
  • Surface Temp5,500 °C
  • Dist. from Earth149.6 M km
  • Age~4.6 billion yrs

Mercury
Terrestrial Planet · 1st from Sun

The smallest planet and the closest to the Sun, Mercury races through space with the shortest year of any planet. It has almost no atmosphere, leaving its surface scarred by craters and subject to extreme temperature swings.

  • Orbital Period87.97 days
  • Dist. from Sun57.9 M km
  • Diameter4,879 km
  • Moons0

Venus
Terrestrial Planet · 2nd from Sun

Often called Earth's twin due to similar size, Venus is shrouded in thick clouds of sulfuric acid and has a runaway greenhouse effect that makes it the hottest planet in the solar system — hotter even than Mercury.

  • Orbital Period224.7 days
  • Dist. from Sun108.2 M km
  • Diameter12,104 km

Earth
Terrestrial Planet · 3rd from Sun

Our home planet — the only known world to harbor life. Earth's liquid water, protective magnetic field, and oxygen-rich atmosphere create the perfect conditions for biology to flourish. One revolution takes exactly 365.25 days.

Jupiter
Gas Giant · 5th from Sun

The largest planet in the solar system, Jupiter is a swirling giant of hydrogen and helium. Its Great Red Spot — a storm larger than Earth — has been raging for centuries. Jupiter's immense gravity acts as a cosmic shield, deflecting many asteroids away from the inner planets.

Saturn
Gas Giant · 6th from Sun

Saturn is the jewel of the solar system — instantly recognizable by its magnificent ring system made of ice and rock. Despite being the second largest planet, Saturn is so low in density that it would float on water. Its rings span 282,000 km but are only about 1 km thick.

  • Orbital Period10,759 days
  • Dist. from Sun1,432 M km
  • Diameter116,460 km

Uranus
Ice Giant · 7th from Sun

Uranus is the oddball of the solar system — it rotates on its side, with its axis tilted at 98 degrees, likely the result of a massive ancient collision. Its blue-green color comes from methane in its atmosphere, which absorbs red light and reflects blue-green wavelengths.

  • Orbital Period30,688 days
  • Dist. from Sun2,867 M km
  • Diameter50,724 km
  • Moons28 known

Neptune
Ice Giant · 8th from Sun

The farthest planet from the Sun, Neptune is a dark and frigid world with the strongest winds in the solar system — reaching speeds of 2,100 km/h. It takes 165 Earth years to complete a single orbit, meaning it has only completed one full trip around the Sun since its discovery in 1846.

  • Orbital Period60,190 days
  • Dist. from Sun4,495 M km
