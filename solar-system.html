About the Project

Our Solar System project is an animated simulation that visualizes the eight planets orbiting the Sun — with attention to relative orbital speeds, planetary sizes, and the characteristic colors and textures of each world. The goal was to create something both scientifically informed and aesthetically captivating — a simulation that teaches while it delights.

The Sun
Star · Center of the Solar System

The Sun is the gravitational anchor of our entire solar system — a massive ball of hot plasma generating energy through nuclear fusion at its core. It accounts for 99.86% of the solar system's total mass and its gravity keeps all eight planets locked in their orbital paths.

  • Diameter1,392,700 km
  • Surface Temp5,500 °C
  • Dist. from Earth149.6 M km
  • Age~4.6 billion yrs

Mercury
Terrestrial Planet · 1st from Sun

The smallest planet and the closest to the Sun, Mercury races through space with the shortest year of any planet. It has almost no atmosphere, leaving its surface scarred by craters and subject to extreme temperature swings.

  • Orbital Period87.97 days
  • Dist. from Sun57.9 M km
  • Diameter4,879 km
  • Moons0

Venus
Terrestrial Planet · 2nd from Sun

Often called Earth's twin due to similar size, Venus is shrouded in thick clouds of sulfuric acid and has a runaway greenhouse effect that makes it the hottest planet in the solar system — hotter even than Mercury.

  • Orbital Period224.7 days
  • Dist. from Sun108.2 M km
  • Diameter12,104 km

Earth
Terrestrial Planet · 3rd from Sun

Our home planet — the only known world to harbor life. Earth's liquid water, protective magnetic field, and oxygen-rich atmosphere create the perfect conditions for biology to flourish. One revolution takes exactly 365.25 days.

  • Orbital Period365.25 days
  • Dist. from Sun149.6 M km
  • Diameter12,742 km
  • Moons1 (The Moon)

Mars
Terrestrial Planet · 4th from Sun

The Red Planet — named for its iron oxide surface — is home to the tallest volcano and the longest canyon in the solar system. Mars is the prime candidate for future human exploration, with evidence of ancient liquid water beneath its dusty plains.

  • Orbital Period686.97 days
  • Dist. from Sun227.9 M km
  • Diameter6,779 km

Jupiter
Gas Giant · 5th from Sun

The largest planet in the solar system, Jupiter is a swirling giant of hydrogen and helium. Its Great Red Spot — a storm larger than Earth — has been raging for centuries. Jupiter's immense gravity acts as a cosmic shield, deflecting many asteroids away from the inner planets.

  • Orbital Period4,332.59 days
  • Dist. from Sun778.5 M km
  • Diameter139,820 km
  • Moons95 known

Saturn
Gas Giant · 6th from Sun

Saturn is the jewel of the solar system — instantly recognizable by its magnificent ring system made of ice and rock. Despite being the second largest planet, Saturn is so low in density that it would float on water. Its rings span 282,000 km but are only about 1 km thick.

  • Orbital Period10,759 days
  • Dist. from Sun1,432 M km
  • Diameter116,460 km
  • Moons146 known

Uranus
Ice Giant · 7th from Sun

Uranus is the oddball of the solar system — it rotates on its side, with its axis tilted at 98 degrees, likely the result of a massive ancient collision. Its blue-green color comes from methane in its atmosphere, which absorbs red light and reflects blue-green wavelengths.

  • Orbital Period30,688 days
  • Dist. from Sun2,867 M km
  • Diameter50,724 km
  • Moons28 known

Neptune
Ice Giant · 8th from Sun

The farthest planet from the Sun, Neptune is a dark and frigid world with the strongest winds in the solar system — reaching speeds of 2,100 km/h. It takes 165 Earth years to complete a single orbit, meaning it has only completed one full trip around the Sun since its discovery in 1846.

  • Orbital Period60,190 days
  • Dist. from Sun4,495 M km
  • Diameter49,244 km
  • Moons16 known
