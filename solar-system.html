About the Project

Our Solar System project is an animated simulation that visualizes the eight planets orbiting the Sun — with attention to relative orbital speeds, planetary sizes, and the characteristic colors and textures of each world. The goal was to create something both scientifically informed and aesthetically captivating — a simulation that teaches while it delights.

Process

How We Built It

01

Research

We studied the real orbital periods and relative sizes of each planet to ensure our simulation was grounded in accurate proportions, even if simplified for visual clarity.

02

Layout

We designed the overall composition — placing the Sun at the center and establishing the elliptical paths each planet would follow throughout the animation.

03

Animation

Each planet was animated along its orbital path at a speed proportional to its real period — Mercury races while Neptune drifts slowly at the edges of the frame.

04

Visual Detailing

We added textures, color gradients, and lighting effects to give each planet its distinctive look — from Jupiter's swirling bands to Saturn's iconic rings.

My Reflection

Building the Solar System animation opened my eyes to the humbling scale of our universe. Getting the orbital speeds and proportional sizes right required real research, and I realized how animation can be both scientifically grounded and aesthetically beautiful. The biggest challenge was making space feel vast without making it feel empty — finding the balance between accuracy and visual engagement. This project made me see animation as a tool not just for storytelling, but for education and wonder.
